# ULRS 1032: Kursus Integriti dan Anti-Rasuah (Minggu Integriti)

## Overview
This directory contains the case study project completed for the **ULRS 1032: Kursus Integriti dan Anti-Rasuah** course during the Undergraduate Studies program. The study, titled **"Penyelewengan di Kementerian Belia dan Sukan (KBS)"**, centers on a critical investigation into a major 2016 government fund misappropriation scandal where a high-ranking divisional secretary acted as the mastermind behind a syndicate that embezzled RM 107 million in public funds. 

## Objectives
* To research and thoroughly analyze the chronological background and critical causes behind the government fund embezzlement at KBS.
* To evaluate the structural role of the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (SPRM) in tackling power abuse, asset recovery, and handling public fund misappropriation.
* To assess the impact of financial corruption on the credibility, accountability, and public trust toward government institutions.
* To conduct an online public perception survey using a Google Form to gather public viewpoints and reactions on systemic corruption and breach of trust.
* To propose actionable preventative measures and legal frameworks aimed at reinforcing internal control mechanisms and an organizational culture of integrity.

## Folder Contents / Evidence
* **`Assignment kajian kes integriti.docx`**: The full written research paper outlining the introduction, literature review on civil service integrity concepts, and data analysis.
* **`Integrity & anticorruption(A2 Poster).pdf`**: The project presentation visual detailing the background, methodology, and graphical survey analysis.
* **`Survey_Data/`**: Collected responses from the online questionnaire deployed via social media platforms.

## What I Did
As part of Section 18, I collaborated with my team members (Dennis Tan Zhi Jie, Liow Jia Feng, and Haviineesh A/L Hariharan) to dissect the financial management flaws of the 2016 KBS incident. We conducted a comprehensive literature review referencing definitions established by the Institut Integriti Malaysia. 

To complement our qualitative research, we designed and deployed a digital survey targeting UTM students. We successfully gathered data from 28 respondents (evenly split between 14 males and 14 females) via WhatsApp and Telegram links. I analyzed the survey outcomes to gauge public consensus regarding structural failures, the efficacy of SPRM's multi-million RM asset seizures, and community expectations for standard punitive actions like heavy fines and imprisonment.

## Key Learning Outcomes
1. **The Core of Civil Service Integrity:** Developed a deep comprehension that civil service integrity demands transparency, strict adherence to laws, accountability, and the total absence of personal conflicts of interest.
2. **Data-Driven Analysis:** Acquired experience in translating raw public survey feedback into scannable empirical data and visual charts mapping public sentiment.
3. **Identifying Risk Multipliers:** Recognized that individual greed, pursuit of luxury, cultural normalization of corruption, and severe lack of internal oversight are main catalysts for massive systemic breaches of trust.
4. **Institutional Awareness:** Understood the extensive operational procedures of bodies like SPRM in tracking down illegal syndicates, freezing hidden bank accounts, and confiscating luxury assets.

## Reflection
Collaborating on this case study provided an eye-opening look at the real-world consequences of compromised professional ethics. While my current primary academic trajectory focuses on engineering data architectures, pipelines, and managing relational databases, this project highlighted a different dimension of data governance: accountability and transparency. 

Seeing how easily multi-million dollar funds were diverted due to weak internal tracking frameworks reinforces my conviction that secure, immutable, and strictly monitored financial data systems are essential. Whether compiling business dashboards or engineering enterprise data infrastructure, establishing strict data integrity and auditable logs mirrors the very compliance structures required to prevent corruption in public governance.

## Possible Improvements
* **Expanding Survey Scope:** The questionnaire relied on a small sample size of 28 UTM students. Expanding the respondent network outside the university circle to include corporate professionals or civil servants would provide a more varied and statistically balanced dataset.
* **Granular Financial System Analysis:** Future iterations of this project could delve deeper into the exact accounting procedures or technical system vulnerabilities that allowed the syndicate to bypass checks undetected for so long.
* **Interactive Data Representation:** Instead of utilizing static poster bar graphs, the survey insights could be rendered into a dynamic, interactive web-based dashboard using modern business intelligence tools to maximize clarity.
