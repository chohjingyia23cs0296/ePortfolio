# Technology and Information System (Year 1, Semester 1)

## Overview
Welcome to the `technology_and_information_system` directory of my ePortfolio. This folder documents the foundational coursework, industrial visits, design thinking projects, and hands-on laboratory sessions from my first semester. This module serves as an introduction to the broader IT landscape, bridging classroom concepts with real-world industry applications and hardware fundamentals.

## Objectives
* To gain a practical understanding of computer hardware architecture through hands-on assembly.
* To explore current industry trends in system development, analytics, and cloud services.
* To connect theoretical classroom learning with real-world technological applications.
* To develop collaborative problem-solving skills through group labs and design thinking methodologies.

## What I Did
Throughout this module, I engaged in a mix of practical labs and industry-facing activities. I worked collaboratively in a lab setting to physically build a computer step-by-step, taking on different roles within my team to understand how individual components interact. Beyond hardware, I attended industrial talks, such as a session hosted by Credence, where I explored system development and learned about industry-standard data tools like PostgreSQL, ClickHouse, Druid, Tableau, and PowerBI. I also participated in a visit to UTM Digital, observing directly how the technological frameworks we study are deployed to manage real-world operations.

## Key Learning Outcomes
1. **Hardware Comprehension:** Gained a tangible, tactile understanding of computer components and architecture through physical assembly.
2. **Industry Tool Familiarity:** Expanded my technical vocabulary and awareness of enterprise-level analytics and cloud technologies.
3. **Collaborative Execution:** Improved teamwork and communication skills by relying on group members during the PC assembly and design thinking processes.
4. **Practical Contextualization:** Successfully linked academic theories to active industry practices and job market realities.

## Reflection
This module was essential in establishing the groundwork for my progression in Data Engineering. The hands-on PC assembly lab provided a concrete understanding of hardware systems, which made it significantly easier to conceptualize virtualized environments and cloud infrastructure later on. The industrial talks were equally impactful; hearing from companies like Credence sparked an initial curiosity in database management and analytics, driving my ongoing focus on building and optimizing robust data pipelines using SQL and cloud platforms. 

## Possible Improvements
Looking back at the experiences documented in this folder, there are a few ways the learning process could be enhanced:
* **Enhanced Interactivity:** For industrial visits (like UTM Digital), incorporating more interactive technologies or storytelling about specific real-world use cases would deepen engagement.
* **Deeper Case Studies:** During industry talks, having speakers delve into specific project challenges and how they overcame them in a dedicated Q&A would provide much stronger practical context.
* **Role Rotation:** In collaborative settings like the PC Assemble lab, rotating roles more frequently during the build process would ensure every team member gains hands-on experience with every phase of the assembly.
