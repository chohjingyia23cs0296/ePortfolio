## Semester Learning
Over Semester 1 I strengthened practical and theoretical skills across AI, data and systems engineering:
- Applied AI: framed problems, designed experiments, trained and evaluated models, and documented empirical results.
- Data modelling & SQL: designed normalized schemas, implemented queries, transactions and procedures, and built database projects end-to-end.
- Systems design & modelling: used UML and architecture patterns to capture requirements, design components and produce deployment-ready documentation.
- Project workflow: wrote proposals, planned deliverables, coordinated work across phases, and prepared final reports and presentations.
- Communication & industry exposure: learned to present technical work and absorbed insights from industry talks integrated into coursework.

## Reflection
Semester 1 balanced disciplined engineering with exploratory experimentation. Artificial Intelligence taught me to make evidence-based design choices (selecting models, hyperparameters and evaluation metrics). Database Programming reinforced the importance of robust schema design, transactional integrity and performant queries for production systems. Enterprise System Development & Modelling emphasised planning, clear documentation and the value of architecture-first thinking when building larger systems.

Key takeaways:
- Start with clear requirements and model evaluation criteria—this focuses experiments and clarifies success.
- Good data/schema design reduces downstream complexity and debugging effort.
- Producing usable artefacts (reports, diagrams, prototypes) and engaging with industry perspectives improved my ability to design practical solutions.
- Timeboxed iteration, thorough documentation and incremental testing made project work more predictable and maintainable.
